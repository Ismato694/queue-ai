# Queue.ai — Business Operating System

**Version:** 1.0
**Type:** Cross-cutting commercial/GTM doc (informs Phase 9 scope, pricing, pilot)
**Anchored to:** Law #0 — *Time is the Product* → **time saved = money saved + money earned**. Baseline-capture mode (R8) turns every modeled number below into the hospital's **real, measured** number.

> ⚠️ **All figures are a transparent model**, not measured results. They use the medium Nigerian private hospital profile (15–40 staff, 5–10 rooms, **~300 visits/day**, 26 operating days/mo = **~7,800 visits/mo**) and conservative assumptions stated inline. Currency ₦. The pilot's first 1–2 weeks (baseline mode) replace these with actuals.

---

## 0. The one-line business case
> A medium hospital loses millions of Naira a month to patients who **abandon long queues** and to **capacity it can't see**. Queue.ai recovers a large share of that for a fraction of the cost — typically a **5–10× monthly ROI** — while making patients, staff, and managers measurably happier.

## 2. Why would they switch from their current process?

Status quo = whiteboard / paper tickets / receptionist memory / "sit and wait." Its hidden costs:

- **Invisible abandonment** — patients leave after long waits and the hospital never knows (lost revenue it can't even measure).
- **Blind capacity** — no one knows a department is about to overload until it has.
- **Receptionist as bottleneck** — manual coordination, errors, disputes over "who's next."
- **Reputation leakage** — "that hospital makes you wait all day" spreads by word of mouth.
- **Zero data** — no basis to staff, schedule, or improve.

**The switch is low-friction by design:** Queue.ai runs **alongside** the existing process (no EMR rip-out, R10), starts in **measure-only baseline mode**, and uses the staff's existing phones/QR. We don't ask them to bet the operation on day one — we prove it in two weeks on their own numbers.

## 4. How much additional REVENUE can Queue.ai generate?

The big lever is **recovering abandoned/no-show visits** + modest throughput gain.

**Assumptions (base case, conservative):**
- 7,800 visits/mo; blended **contribution per visit ₦12,000** (consult + ancillary lab/pharmacy share — hospitals vary widely; set to actuals in baseline).
- Current loss rate (abandon + no-show that doesn't rebook) **8%** → ~624 lost visits/mo.
- Queue.ai cuts that loss by **~40% relative** (8% → ~4.8%) → **~250 visits/mo recovered**.

| Source | Calc | Monthly revenue (base) |
|--------|------|------------------------|
| **Recovered abandonment/no-show** | 250 × ₦12,000 | **₦3.0M** |

| Retention/referral uplift (LTV) | reputation → repeat visits | upside, longer-horizon |
| **Revenue subtotal (conservative)** | | **~₦3.0M+/mo** |

**Sensitivity (revenue recovered only):**
| Scenario | Loss rate cut | Visits recovered/mo | Revenue/mo |
|----------|---------------|---------------------|-----------|

## 9. How do we onboard a hospital in ONE DAY?

**The One-Day Playbook (single branch):**

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| Pre-visit (remote) | Create org/branch, import services, pick/customize Flow template, generate branch QR, provision staff accounts |
| **08:00–09:00** | Arrive; confirm flow with manager; place QR signage; set up reception device + UPS check |
| 09:00–10:30 | Train receptionist hands-on; do 5 live test patients |
| 10:30–12:00 | **Go live in baseline mode** (measure current waits, low intervention); shadow reception |
| 12:00–13:00 | Train staff (10 min each, room by room); manager walkthrough |
| 13:00–16:00 | Live operation with on-site support; tune service durations from real data |
| 16:00–17:00 | Manager dashboard session; set notification budgets; agree success metrics; leave quick-cards + support line |
| Day-end | Branch is **live**; baseline measuring; support on call for week 1 |

Keys to one-day success: **no EMR integration required to start**, staff use existing phones, paper fallback stays available as a safety net.

---

## 10. How do we migrate from paper queues?

**Parallel run, never big-bang.**
1. **Week 0–1:** Queue.ai runs *alongside* paper. Receptionist enters walk-ins into Queue.ai (or patients self-QR); **paper kept as fallback** (R5).
2. Baseline mode records the "before" numbers (proof + Org Memory seed).
3. **Week 2:** Queue.ai becomes primary; paper is the emergency-only backup (offline outages).
4. Returning-patient quick-lookup (Queue Passport single-org, F9) speeds re-registration.
No historical data entry required — we start clean and let the system learn.

## 11–14. Objection Handling

> Principle: every objection is answered with **proof on their own numbers** (baseline) + **risk removal** (parallel run, no rip-out, pilot pricing).

### 11. Administrators / Owners (the buyer)
| Objection | Rebuttal |
|-----------|----------|
| "Too expensive." | All-in ~₦360k/mo vs modeled ₦3M+ recovered/mo. We **prove it on your baseline before you commit**; pilot onboarding free. |
| "We already have a system / whiteboard." | It runs **alongside** — no rip-out (R10). The whiteboard can't show you the revenue you're losing to abandonment; we measure it. |
| "Will my staff actually use it?" | Receptionist training <90 min; staff <15 min; it's **faster than today by lunch**, or we keep iterating. |
| "Data privacy / NDPR?" | NDPR-aligned architecture, encryption, audit logs, names never on public screens (R3). |
| "What if the internet/power fails?" | **Offline-first** reception + SMS + paper fallback (R5) — designed for Nigerian conditions. |
| "Prove the ROI." | Baseline mode produces *your* before/after in weeks; dashboard shows time saved & Flow Score live. |

### 12. Receptionists (the daily operator — adoption gatekeeper)
| Objection | Rebuttal |
|-----------|----------|
| "It'll slow me down / more work." | Add-walk-in is **<3 taps**; calling next is one tap; it removes manual list-keeping. Net **time saved**, not added. |
| "I'll get blamed when it breaks." | Offline mode keeps you working; conflicts are flagged, not punished; paper fallback always there. |
| "I don't trust computers for this." | Hands-on training + on-site support week 1; you stay in control (you call next, not the machine). |
| "Patients will be confused." | Patients mostly self-serve via QR/SMS, **reducing** your interruptions. |

### 13. Doctors / Clinical staff
| Objection | Rebuttal |
|-----------|----------|
| "I'm not tapping buttons all day." | Your view is **one card, one tap** (done/transfer); the nurse/receptionist can drive state. Minimal interaction by design (ADM-4). |
| "FIFO is wrong for medicine." | It's **not** FIFO — acuity/triage drives order; emergencies preempt; every override is logged (R2). You stay clinically in charge. |
| "It'll rush my consultations." | It never imposes a time limit on you; it predicts, it doesn't police. Durations are seeds, not targets. |
| "More admin overhead." | It **reduces** overhead — no chasing who's next; auto-advances the pathway. |

### 14. Patients
| Objection | Rebuttal |
|-----------|----------|
| "I don't have a smartphone / data." | Walk-in + **SMS** + receptionist are first-class; no app needed (R5). |
| "Will I lose my place if I'm not there?" | **Pre-Queue + grace window** protect your spot; you're told exactly when to leave (R4). |
| "I don't trust the time estimate." | **Trust Engine** shows confidence *and the reasons*; honest ranges, never fake promises (F11). |
| "Is my data safe?" | Minimal data, NDPR-aligned, your name never shown on public screens (R3). |
| "This is complicated." | One screen: where you are, what's next, when. Designed to *remove* effort, not add it. |
